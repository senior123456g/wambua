Lesson on Landforms

Landforms are natural features of the Earth's surface. They include mountains, valleys, plateaus, hills, plains, and more.

Some common types of landforms are:

  • Mountains: Large landforms that rise prominently above their surroundings.
  • Valleys: Low areas of land between hills or mountains, often with rivers running through them.
  • Plateaus: Elevated flat areas that are often surrounded by steep cliffs.
  • Hills: Smaller elevations of the Earth's surface, typically not as tall as mountains.
  • Plains: Large areas of flat or gently rolling land, often with fertile soil.
  • Deserts: Arid, dry regions with little vegetation and little rainfall.
  • Canyons: Deep, narrow valleys with steep sides, often carved by rivers over time.
