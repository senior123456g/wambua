Characteristics of Different Landforms

1. Mountains

Height and Elevation: Mountains are typically higher than surrounding landforms, often rising hundreds to thousands of meters above sea level.

2. Valleys

Shape: Valleys are typically low-lying areas between mountains or hills, often shaped by rivers or glaciers.

3. Deserts

Dry Climate: Deserts are characterized by extreme dryness and receive less than 10 inches of rain per year.

4. Plateaus

Flat Terrain: Plateaus are flat or gently sloping elevated areas that have been uplifted by tectonic forces.

5. Rivers

Flowing Water: Rivers are natural watercourses that flow toward oceans, lakes, or other rivers.
